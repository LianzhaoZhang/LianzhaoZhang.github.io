### Project Overview

- Laboratory Research Project
- Duration: January 2024 - December 2024
- Research Motivation: The basic dynamic locomotion capabilities of humanoid robots serve as the foundation for executing subsequent tasks. This project focused on developing dynamic motion control algorithms for the Kuavo Gen-3 humanoid robot manufactured by LimX Dynamics, enabling the robot to perform squatting and dynamic walking.

### Main Contributions

- Developed center of mass dynamics model, using Model Predictive Control (MPC) to plan CoM trajectory and optimal foothold positions
- Implemented whole-body control to compute joint commands that track desired trajectories while satisfying physical constraints
- Applied Extended Kalman Filter for robot state estimation
